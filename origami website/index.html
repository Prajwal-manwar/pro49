Facts about camels

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.

Facts about Chameleon

  • Almost half of all known species live in Madagascar.
  • They mainly change color in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.
  • Unlike many lizards, chameleons can’t regrow their tails.

Facts about Pigeons

  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph.

Facts about pandas

  • They have an extended wrist bone that they use like a thumb to help them grip food.
  • Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo.
  • Pandas need at least 2 different bamboo species in their range to avoid starvation

Facts about bears

  • There are around 180,000 to 200,000 brown bears worldwide.
  • Brown bears in the wild are mainly active at dusk and at night.
  • The brown bear is the largest predator still living on the continent of Europe.

Facts about Fliying-Cicada

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  • Cicadidae can be found on every continent except Antarctica.
